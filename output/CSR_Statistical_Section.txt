Study ABC-mAb-101 | ABCmAb Monoclonal Antibody | Phase I Single-Dose PK
Prepared for: Clinical Study Report (CSR)

1. METHODS

  1.1 Study Design

  This was an open-label, single-dose pharmacokinetic study of ABCmAb
  administered subcutaneously at 10 mg/kg in 22 healthy adult volunteers.
  Nominal blood sampling occurred at 0, 24, 48, 72, 96, 168, 336, 504, 672,
  840, and 1008 hours post-dose.

  1.2 Analyte and Bioanalytical Method

  The primary analyte was the ABCmAb parent compound measured in plasma.
  An exploratory metabolite was assessed in parallel (results not presented
  here). Concentrations are reported in µg/mL.

  1.3 NCA Methodology

  Non-compartmental analysis was conducted using the linear-up/log-down
  (linear-log) trapezoidal rule, consistent with FDA and EMA guidance.
  Pre-dose BLQ values were set to zero (M1 rule). Post-dose BLQ values
  were set to zero for AUC calculation.

  The terminal elimination rate constant (kel) was estimated by log-linear
  regression over the last four quantifiable time points per participant.
  A minimum adjusted R² of 0.90 was required for kel estimation. AUC0-inf
  was calculated as AUC0-t + Clast/kel.

  All parameters were calculated at the individual level prior to summary.
  Descriptive statistics include N, arithmetic mean, standard deviation (SD),
  coefficient of variation (CV%), median, minimum, and maximum.
  tmax is presented as median [range] per convention.

2. RESULTS

  2.1 Participant Disposition

  Twenty-two participants were enrolled, dosed, and included in the PK
  analysis set: 12 male and 10 female. Mean age was 39.3 years (range:
  27–55) and mean body weight was 71.4 kg (range: 56–88).

  2.2 Plasma Concentration-Time Profiles

  Following subcutaneous administration of ABCmAb at 10 mg/kg, plasma
  concentrations were below the limit of quantification at time zero and
  increased progressively across all participants, reaching a peak at a
  median tmax of 168 hours (range: 168–168 h) post-dose.
  Concentrations declined in a mono-exponential manner over the
  elimination phase. Individual and mean (±SD) concentration-time profiles
  are presented in Figures 1–4.

  2.3 Primary PK Parameters

  The mean (SD) Cmax was 52.58 (5.75) µg/mL (CV% 10.9%).
  Mean (SD) AUC0-t was 31197.0 (4100.0) h·µg/mL (CV% 13.1%).
  Mean (SD) AUC0-inf was 34575.7 (5135.1) h·µg/mL (CV% 14.9%).
  Mean AUC extrapolation was 9.55% (range: 6.66–12.27%), indicating
  adequate sampling duration for AUC0-inf estimation.

  2.4 Secondary PK Parameters

  Mean (SD) terminal half-life (t½) was 257.1 (19.2) hours, consistent
  with the extended circulation expected for a monoclonal antibody.
  Mean (SD) MRT was 492.0 (23.4) hours.
  Mean (SD) apparent clearance was 0.0207 (0.0006) mL/h.
  All kel estimates met the minimum adjusted R² threshold of 0.90
  (mean adj. R² = 0.9859, range: 0.9741–0.9923).

  2.5 Sex Differences

  Mean Cmax in male participants was 57.37 µg/mL vs 47.78 µg/mL in female
  participants. Mean AUC0-t was 34625.1 vs 27768.9 h·µg/mL respectively.
  Differences reflect weight-based dosing (10 mg/kg); dose-normalised
  parameters show reduced between-sex variability (see Table 3).

3. CONCLUSIONS

  ABCmAb demonstrated predictable subcutaneous absorption with consistent
  individual profiles across all 22 participants. Between-subject variability
  in Cmax and AUC0-t (CV% 11% and 13% respectively) was within the
  range expected for a monoclonal antibody at this dose level. Terminal
  half-life estimates were consistent with the known FcRn-mediated recycling
  mechanism of IgG-class molecules. Full parameter tabulations are provided
  in Tables 2 and 3; individual data are in Listings 1 and 2.
